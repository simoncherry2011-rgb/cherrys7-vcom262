So, You Want To Learn How To Find A Phage?

Well then, let’s start with Isolation!

Collection

Phages can be found just about anywhere---including the dirt!.

Bacteria tend to live in wet/moist soil, so collecting dirt is a great place to start.

Direct Isolation

Here, we separate the phages from the bacteria and other muck, leaving us with a liquid sample of what hopefully contains phages.

Plaque Assays and Spot Tests

These tests help us detect the presence of phages within our samples.

You can tell that there are phages when little plaques appear on the petri dish. That’s a sign of phages bursting the host cell.
