Amplification

In order to study a phage, we need it's DNA. But we have a problem: phages barely have any DNA

So here's the solution: Make a lot of phages

Webbed Plate

Our goal is to cultivate enough phages to be study their DNA. Our method of doing this can be seen below:

Webbed Plate

At this point, the phages on the lawn have lysed nearly all of the bacteria on the play. All of the tiny holed leave ony threads of bacteria remaining (hence the name "Webbed Plate")

Hover Over Image

Lysate

To collect our phage, we flood our webbed plates with a special liquid. We then suck up that liquid. The Final result is called a phage lysate.

We then repeat this process over and over again until we have enough for our final stage: Extraction.
